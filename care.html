Preserve of Herbarium

A herbarium specimen must be preserved using a number of procedures to guarantee that the plants are mounted, dried, and kept in a way that will preserve their aesthetic and scientific worth for many years. This is a how-to for successfully preserving specimens in herbariums:

Environment Control


  • Maintain the herbarium in a climate-controlled space with consistent temperatures (preferably between 15¬∞C and 20¬∞C) and low humidity (below 50%). Excessive humidity can promote the growth of mould, and excessive temperatures can degrade specimens.
  • An good air circulation can help moisture accumulation.
  • Store herbarium in low light or dark environments to prevent the plant pigments from fading due to light.

Storage of Herbarium


  • Using metal storage cabinets with tightly fitting door to keep pests like insects away from the herbarium.
  • Keep herbarium sheets in boxes or folders of archival quality that are free of acid to avoid paper deterioration over time.
  • Cover delicate or fragile specimens with sleeves made of acid-free plastic to give them further defence against dust, pests, and physical harm.

Insect and Pest Management


  • For pest-free storage, place dried plant specimens in a plastic bag that is tightly sealed. You can freeze the herbarium for three or four days to kill insects.
  • Using non-toxic natural repellents and put it around the herbarium to prevent the insect and pest.

Fungal and Mold Prevention


  • Put desiccants, such as silica gel packets, in plastic bags or storage cabinets to absorb excess moisture and lower the chance of fungus growth.
  • Regular inspection of herbarium check about got any mold or fungal. If contaminated specimens are found, separate them and clean them with a soft brush or alcohol solution while making sure the storage space is dry.

Handling Guide


  • When handling herbarium, grasp both sides of the sheet.
  • when transporting specimens, set them on a piece of cardboard. Specimens should never be bent and should always be kept horizontal.
  • The herbarium must be facing up to avoid stacking with other herbarium was facing down and flip them together like page in a book.
